##### **block and inline elements :**

* A block-level element always starts on a new line and takes up the full width available

A. Page Structure Elements

* header , main , section , div , footer , article and aside

B. Text Blocks

* h1 , p , hr , br ,pre and blackquote

C. Grouping

* table , ol , ul , li , dd , dt , dl ,figure , figcaption and fieldset 

D. Others  

* address , form and videos , canvas and noscript

* An inline element does not start on a new line and it only takes up as much width as necessary

A. Text Formatting

* bold , strong , italic , em , small , big , q , abbr , acronym , cite , span , sub , sup , code , var

B. Input/Control

* label , input/output , textarea , button and select

C. Embedded items

* map , image , script and object

* The <div> element is a block-level element and is often used as

a container for other HTML elements

* The <span> element is an inline container used to mark up a part of a text, or a part of a document
