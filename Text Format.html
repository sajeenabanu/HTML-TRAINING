Romeo and Juliet is Shakespeare's tragic story of two young lovers from feuding families, the Montagues and Capulets, in Verona, Italy, who fall deeply in love, marry secretly, but are doomed by misunderstandings and fate, leading to their suicides, which ultimately ends their families' bitter feud. After a street brawl, Romeo attends a Capulet party, meets Juliet, and they fall instantly in love, marrying with help from Friar Laurence. Romeo's banishment for killing Juliet's cousin, Tybalt, leads to Juliet faking her death with a potion, but Romeo, not getting the message, believes her truly dead, drinks poison, and Juliet stabs herself upon waking to find him gone, uniting their families in grief.
